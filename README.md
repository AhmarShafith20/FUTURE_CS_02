# Phishing Email Detection & Awareness System

## 📌 Project Overview

This project is part of the **Future Interns Cyber Security Internship – Task 2 (2026)**.

The goal is to analyze phishing email samples, identify common attack patterns, and build awareness around how phishing attacks work in real-world scenarios.

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or financial details by impersonating trusted organizations.

---

## 🎯 Objective

* Analyze multiple phishing email samples
* Identify phishing indicators and attack techniques
* Examine email headers for authenticity
* Classify email risk levels
* Understand social engineering strategies
* Create awareness and prevention guidelines

---

## 🧪 Email Samples Analyzed

This project includes analysis of three simulated phishing emails:

1. **Bank Account Alert Scam** – Fake banking security warning
2. **IT Helpdesk Password Reset Scam** – Corporate credential harvesting attempt
3. **PayPal Invoice Scam** – Fake payment and refund fraud email

Each email was evaluated for:

* Sender authenticity
* Domain legitimacy
* Header security checks (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
* Suspicious links
* Social engineering tactics

---

## ⚠️ Key Phishing Indicators Identified

* Fake or spoofed sender domains
* Urgent or threatening language
* Requests for sensitive information
* Suspicious URLs or redirect links
* Poor authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC failures)
* Generic greetings (e.g., “Dear User”)
* Impersonation of trusted brands

---

## 📊 Risk Classification

All analyzed emails were classified as:

**🔴 HIGH RISK – PHISHING**

These emails show strong indicators of social engineering attacks designed to steal credentials or financial information.

---

## 🛡️ Prevention Guidelines

### ✔ Do’s

* Verify sender email addresses carefully
* Hover over links before clicking
* Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Report suspicious emails
* Confirm requests via official channels

### ❌ Don’ts

* Do not click unknown links
* Do not share passwords or OTPs
* Do not trust urgent requests blindly
* Do not download unexpected attachments
